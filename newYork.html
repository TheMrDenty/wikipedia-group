New York (state)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

New Yorkofficially known as the State of New York, is a state in the Northeastern United States It is often called New York State to distinguish it from its largest city, New York City. With a total area of 54,556 square miles (141,300 km2),[2] New York is the 27th-largest U.S. state by area. With 20.2 million people, it is the fourth-most-populous state in the United States as of 2021, with approximately 44% living in New York City, including 25% of the state's population within Brooklyn and Queens, and another 15% on the remainder of Long Island, the most populous island in the United States.[6][9] The state is bordered by New Jersey and Pennsylvania to the south, and Connecticut, Massachusetts, and Vermont to the east; it has a maritime border with Rhode Island, east of Long Island, as well as an international border with the Canadian provinces of Quebec to the north and Ontario to the northwest.
